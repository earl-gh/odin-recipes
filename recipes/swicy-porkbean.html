Swicy Porkbean

Description

Chinese Stir-Fried Green Beans with Minced Pork, also known as Roumo Jiangdou, is a term for a flavorful serving of finely chopped green beans stir-fried with ground pork in a sweet and spicy sauce. It is a popular, comforting meal particularly associated with home-cooked Chinese cuisine or casual diner-style eateries where it is served as a "xia fan cai" (rice-killer) dish over steamed white rice. Source: Google Search

Personal Note: This is a personal recipe rendition by the Filipino learner who created this webpage. After eating sitaw prepared the same way for several days, he decided to break the monotony by cooking it differently from the usual adobo or soup-based dishes. Wanting to make the ingredient enticing again for his small family, he experimented to create this specific version. The final dish brings a completely new flavor profile to the table, perfectly hitting the mark with a satisfying balance of rich sweetness and a lively kick of spice.

Servings: Yields 2 to 3 servings (ideal for a family of 6 when paired with rice and side dishes).

Ingredients

  • 500g Ground pork
  • 300g Snake beans (sitaw), minced
  • 2 tbsp Unsalted butter or margarine
  • 3 pieces Bird's eye chili (sili labuyo), minced
  • 2 pieces Banana chili (siling pansigang), sliced crosswise
  • 3 tbsp White sugar
  • 1/4 cup Oyster sauce

Steps

Preparation

  1. Mince the snake beans into small, uniform pieces to match the size of the ground pork.
  2. Finely chop the bird's eye chilies and slice the banana chilies into thin rings.
  3. Measure and portion all remaining ingredients before cooking.

Cooking

  1. Melt the butter or margarine in a wok or large skillet over high heat.
  2. Sauté the minced bird's eye chilies and sliced banana chilies briefly until highly fragrant.
  3. Add the ground pork to the pan, breaking it apart continuously to ensure a fine texture, and sauté until browned.
  4. Incorporate the minced snake beans into the pan, stir-frying for a few minutes until crisp-tender and vibrant green.
  5. Deglaze the pan by pouring in the oyster sauce and tossing thoroughly to coat the pork and beans.
  6. Sprinkle the white sugar over the mixture, stirring constantly until the sugar caramelizes and glazes the ingredients.
  7. Reduce the heat and let the dish simmer for another minute until the sauce thickens and coats everything evenly.

Serving Suggestions

  • Ladle hot into a shallow serving bowl.
  • Serve alongside a mountain of garlic fried rice to balance the rich sauce.
  • Pair with ice-cold soft drinks or your choice of cold beverage to complement the spicy kick.
